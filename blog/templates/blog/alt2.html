Tax implications: Tax implications arising from the acquisition of a real estate property in Kefalonia.

Acquisition (Same as Alternative A):

  • RETT
    => RETT + Municipality Duty = 6,180 euros
  • Notary Fees = 1.23% x 200,000 = 2,460 euros
  • Greek Land Registry Fees = 0.52% x 200,000 = 1,040 euros
    or Greek Cadastre = 0.63% x 200,000 = 1,260 euros

Note: Foreign individuals must have a Greek Tax Registration Number and file a Real Estate Property Data Declaration (E9) within a month after the purchase of the house.

Holding:

  • ENFIA (Same as Alternative A)
    => ENFIA = + 450euros/year x 5years = 2,250 euros
  • Municipality Duty (Same as Alternative A)
    => Municipality Duty = 500euros/year x 5 years = 2,500 euros
  • Duty for cleaning and lighting services offered by the municipality and an extra tax on electrified spaces determined by the municipal council
  • Maintenance & Refurbishment Works (Same as Alternative A): the VAT is equal to 23% x 50,000 = 11,500 euros
  • Greek Accounting Standards: According to Greek Accounting Standards, foreign corporations have obligations of keeping books and records when they exercise a business activity in Greece. Assuming that the Foreign Co. leases the property and generates income in Greece, it should comply with all obligations of L.4301/2014
  • Special Real Estate Tax: A special real estate tax is imposed annually at a rate of 15% on the objective value of the building.

Note: Exemption from the above mentioned special real estate tax may be available for an individual shareholder that has a Greek tax registration number, after submitting a specific set of documentation on an annual basis.

Income Taxation:

  • Free concession of use of the house: Deemed income arising from the free concession of the property is calculated at 3% of the buildingâ€™s objective value and applies only to the individual, not the Foreign Co. Assuming that this this benefit is considered as employment income, it is taxable at a scale of 42% and as a result, the annual tax would be
    => Free concession tax = 42% x (3% x 200,000)euros/year x 5years = 12,600 euros
  • Rental income: The income derived from a property located in Greece is a Greek Source Income and it is taxable in Greece. In our case, if the Foreign Co. leases the house to a 3d party, this business activity is taxed at a rate of 26%.

Note: Free concession issue should be further clarified by the Greek Authorities. However, in order to bypass this uncertainty, the Foreign Co. should have a lease agreement with the individual.

Note: According to the provisions of article 5 of the ITC, any business income arising of a Foreign Co. in Greece, is taxable only if it maintains a permanent establishment (PE) in Greece. Moreover, Greek Tax Authorities have adopted a position in the past that a Foreign Co. that leases a property without being engaged to any other business activity in Greece, does not constitute a PE. As a result, there are grounded arguments to support that the rental income should not be subject to tax.

Selling or Transferring the House:

Selling:

Based on the analysis above, any income arising for the Foreign Co. from selling the property to a 3d party is not considered taxable unless the Greek Authorities consider that the Foreign Co. has a Permanent Establishment (PE) in Greece - where a tax of 26% for business activities is applied.

Sale of shares:

No tax implications arise in the case that both the individual and the Foreign Co. are foreign tax residents.

Inheritance:

In case that the children/wife/parents of the individual have not Greek nationality or the individual has not his residence in Greece, the inheritance-donation of Foreign Co. shares by the individual to his children/wife/parents falls outside the scope of Greek inheritance tax.
